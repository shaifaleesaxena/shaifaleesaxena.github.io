## Current research directions

### Reinforcement learning and bounded extremum seeking for time-varying systems

I am interested in hybrid learning-and-control methods that improve the robustness of reinforcement learning controllers when system dynamics vary over time. This line of work combines fast learned policies with online adaptation from bounded extremum seeking.

### Robust robotic manipulation under distribution shift

My recent work studies robotic pushing and pick-and-place when test-time conditions differ from training conditions. The goal is to improve performance under changes such as time-varying goals, contact conditions, and spatially varying dynamics.

## Prior work and engineering projects

### Flight-control simulation and EFCS development

At Airbus, I worked on electronic flight-control system packages for aircraft simulators, validation workflows, malfunction implementation, and technical support for pilot-training programs.

### UAV autopilot, estimation, and control

At Tata Advanced Systems, I worked on 6-DOF aircraft modeling, trim analysis, control-law design, total energy control systems, pitch and roll hold loops, and extended Kalman filter based estimation for autopilot systems.

### Structural health monitoring with guided waves

My undergraduate research studied online damage detection in doubly curved shells using time-reversed guided waves. This work led to a conference paper and a second-best-paper award.

### Optimization and modeling projects

Other past projects include vehicle dynamics modeling and control, quadrotor simulation and control, and optimization formulations such as the multiple traveling salesman problem.
