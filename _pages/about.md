I am a second-year PhD student working at the intersection of **robotics, reinforcement learning, and control**. My recent work focuses on robust decision-making under distribution shift, adaptive control for time-varying systems, and robotic manipulation.

Before beginning my PhD, I worked in aerospace and autonomous systems engineering, including flight-control simulation and control development in industry. That background still shapes how I think about research: I am especially interested in methods that are both **theoretically grounded** and **deployable on real systems**.

## Research interests

- Reinforcement learning for robotics and control
- Robust and adaptive control
- Learning and adaptation under distribution shift
- Robotic manipulation
- Estimation and system identification
- Aerospace and autonomous systems

## Background

My academic and professional background spans **aerospace engineering**, **flight-control systems**, **autopilot design**, **state estimation**, and **robotics**. Prior to graduate school, I worked on problems involving electronic flight control systems, UAV modeling, control-law development, extended Kalman filtering, system identification, and simulation.

I completed my B.Tech. in **Aerospace Engineering** at the **Indian Institute of Space Science and Technology (IIST)**.
